# Style Guide Reference

## Brand Voice at a Glance

| Attribute     | Do                                   | Don't                                    |
|---------------|--------------------------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Voice         | Friendly, direct, clear              | Formal, stiff, bureaucratic              |
| Tone          | Positive, inclusive, energetic       | Pushy, condescending, negative           |
| Sentences     | Short to medium (≤ 25 words)         | Run-ons with many clauses                |
| Vocabulary    | Plain English, common words          | Buzzwords, jargon, Latin phrases         |
| Point of view | "you" for readers, "we" for company  | "one", "users", "customers" (impersonal) |
| Contractions  | Encouraged ("you're", "we've")       | Overly formal ("you are", "we have")     |

## Content Types

### Emails

**Subject lines**
- 40–60 characters
- State the benefit or action directly
- Avoid "Re:", "FWD:", "Important:"

**Body**
- One idea per paragraph (3–5 sentences max)
- Lead with the most important information (inverted pyramid)
- CTA button label: verb phrase ("Get started", "See your report", "Download now")
- Sign-off: "Best," or "Thanks," — avoid "Sincerely" or "Regards"

**Tone dial**
- Transactional: neutral and informative
- Marketing: warm and benefit-focused
- Support: empathetic, solution-first

---

### Blog Posts

**Structure**
- H1: main title (≤ 70 chars for SEO)
- H2: major sections (3–7 per post)
- H3: sub-points
- Keep intro under 100 words; hook in sentence 1

**Style**
- Write at 8th-grade reading level (Flesch-Kincaid ~60–70)
- Define acronyms on first use: "search engine optimization (SEO)"
- Paragraph length: 3–4 sentences for desktop; 1–2 for mobile-first content

**Headings**
- Use sentence case ("How to write faster") not title case ("How To Write Faster")
- Make headings outcome-focused, not label-focused

---

### Social Media

**LinkedIn**
- Professional but personable
- 1,300 characters or fewer for full visibility
- Open with a hook (question, stat, or bold claim) — not "I'm excited to share"
- Line breaks after every 1–2 sentences for readability

**Twitter / X**
- 240 characters or fewer
- Active voice only
- Use 1 hashtag maximum (industry or campaign tag)
- Threads: lead tweet must stand alone

**Instagram**
- Caption opens with a strong visual description or question
- Hashtags at end of caption, max 10 relevant tags
- Emojis: max 3–5, only if they add meaning

---

### Support / Help Content

**Tone**
- Empathetic first ("That sounds frustrating — let's fix it.")
- Solution-first structure: answer first, explanation second
- Never blame the user or imply user error
- Use "let's" to partner with the user ("Let's get this sorted.")

**Format**
- Numbered lists for sequential steps
- Bold for UI elements: click **Save**, press **Ctrl + Z**
- Code blocks for commands, paths, or exact strings
- End with a next-step offer: "Does that resolve it, or would you like me to dig deeper?"

## Grammar & Mechanics

### Punctuation
- **Oxford comma**: "red, white, and blue" ✅ / "red, white and blue" ❌
- **Em dash**: for parenthetical emphasis — no spaces around it
- **Exclamation marks**: maximum one per piece of content; prefer zero

### Capitalization
- Sentence case for headings, button labels, and navigation items
- Title case only for: product names, legal documents, formal titles
- "internet", "web", "email" are lowercase

### Numbers
- Spell out: zero through nine
- Digits: 10 and above, percentages ("12%"), decimals ("3.5"), ranges ("2–4")
- Never start a sentence with a digit; rewrite the sentence

### Inclusive Language
- Use "they/them" as singular pronoun when gender is unknown
- "Allowlist / blocklist" over "whitelist / blacklist"
- Avoid ableist phrasing: "see the docs" → "check the docs"
