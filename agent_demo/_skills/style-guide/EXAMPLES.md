# Style Guide Examples

## Email Subject Lines

| Before (❌)                           | After (✅)                          | Rule Applied                        |
|--------------------------------------|------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| "Important update regarding account" | "Your account: action needed"      | Lead with the what, not "important" |
| "We are pleased to announce…"        | "Introducing [Feature]: [benefit]" | Cut filler, lead with value         |
| "October Newsletter"                 | "5 things we shipped in October"   | Specific beats generic              |
| "Following up on our conversation"   | "Quick follow-up on [topic]"       | Name the topic; cut hollow openers  |

## Email Body Rewrites

### Support Email — Before

> Dear Customer, We are writing to inform you that your recent support ticket #12345
> has been received and is currently being processed by our technical team. We appreciate
> your patience during this time and will endeavor to respond within 2 business days.

### Support Email — After

> Hi [Name], we've got your ticket (#12345) and our team is on it. You'll hear back
> within 2 business days — often sooner. Thanks for your patience.

**Changes**: "Dear Customer" → first name; passive voice removed; "we are" → "we've"; added human closer.

|--------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| "Introduction to Content Marketing"  | "What is content marketing (and why it works)" |

## Common Jargon → Plain English

| Jargon (❌)              | Plain English (✅)               |
|-------------------------|---------------------------------|
| "leverage synergies"    | "work together"                 |
| "circle back"           | "follow up"                     |
| "move the needle"       | "make a difference"             |
| "utilize"               | "use"                           |
| "at this point in time" | "now"                           |
| "end-to-end solution"   | "tool that handles everything"  |
| "best-in-class"         | "one of the best" (or prove it) |
| "deep dive"             | "look closely at"               |

## Social Media Rewrites

### LinkedIn — Before

> Excited to share that we have just launched our new feature! We have been working on
> it for months and are really pleased with the results. Check it out!

### LinkedIn — After

> We just shipped [Feature Name] — and it does something we've wanted for a long time:
> [one-sentence benefit].
>
> Here's what it means for you:
> - [Concrete outcome 1]
> - [Concrete outcome 2]
> - [Concrete outcome 3]
>
> Learn more → [link]

**Changes**: Removed "excited to share"; opened with the thing, not the feeling; bullet outcomes; replaced vague "check
it out" with a CTA.

---

### Twitter — Before

> We are happy to announce the launch of our amazing new product that helps teams
> collaborate more effectively across the organization!

### Twitter — After

> [Product] is live. [One-sentence value prop] → [link]

**Changes**: Cut to under 140 chars; active present-tense verb; single clear link.

## Support Reply Rewrites

### Tone — Before

> You need to clear your cache and cookies and then try again. This is a common
> issue that users experience.

### Tone — After

> Let's clear your cache and cookies — that usually solves it. Here's how:
>
> 1. [Step one]
> 2. [Step two]
>
> Reply here if it's still happening and we'll dig deeper.

**Changes**: "you need to" → "let's"; solution-first with numbered steps; empathetic close.
